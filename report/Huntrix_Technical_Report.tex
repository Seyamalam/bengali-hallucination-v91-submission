\begin{abstract}
We present a selective cascade for detecting hallucinations in Bengali model responses. Instead of asking one model to judge every row, the system first applies exact source-lineage checks, task-specific solvers, context grounding, and relation-typed evidence. A quantized open-weight language model judges only unresolved cases. This design raised public macro-F1 from 0.657 to 0.967 and obtained private macro-F1 0.982, ranking first in Phase 1. On 299 released labels, the full cascade reaches 0.9933 macro-F1; deterministic stages cover 225 rows without error. Three offline Kaggle runs on two Tesla T4 GPUs reproduced the scored 2,516-row output byte for byte in 440.87--593.43 seconds. A 5,000-row same-mix run took 14.60 minutes, while an all-uncertain stress estimate remained below 5.5 hours. The study shows that careful evidence admission, explicit abstention, and deterministic scheduling can outperform uniform LLM judging while satisfying a nine-hour offline inference limit.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

The Bengali LLM Hallucination Detection Challenge asks whether a candidate response is fabricated, unsupported, or factually wrong (\labelzero), or faithful (\labelone). A row contains a Bengali prompt, a response, and sometimes a supporting context. The released development set has 299 labels; Phase 1 contains 2,516 unlabeled rows. The held-out Phase 2 set contains approximately 5,000 rows from a wider range of sources \citep{competition,phase2}.

This is not one homogeneous classification problem. The benchmark mixes context entailment, arithmetic, multiple-choice questions, morphology, idioms, legislation, history, literature, and civic information. It also inherits task diversity from Bengali hallucination research such as BenHalluEval \citep{benhallu}. A generic LLM prompt must solve all of these relations with one evidence policy, and our early judges were both prompt-sensitive and expensive.

Our central decision was therefore to make the classifier selective. A deterministic component may label a row only when its scope, evidence source, and semantic relation are explicit. Otherwise it abstains and sends the row to a bounded LLM judge. This separation gives exact tasks to exact methods and reserves model capacity for genuine ambiguity. The final system has three goals: high recall for the hallucinated class, protection against semantically related but irrelevant retrieval, and reproducible offline execution on Kaggle T4$\times$2.

\section{Method}

Figure~\ref{fig:architecture} shows the frozen v91 cascade. For input row $x$, the router returns either a label $d(x)\in\{0,1\}$ with provenance or an abstention symbol $\bot$. The final prediction is
\[
\hat{y}(x)=
\begin{cases}
d(x), & d(x)\neq\bot,\\
J(x), & d(x)=\bot,
\end{cases}
\]
where $J$ is a fixed-order, grammar-constrained open-weight judge.

\subsection{Normalization and Routing}

The first stage normalizes Bengali and Latin digits, Unicode punctuation, spacing, answer aliases, and inline answer options. It also distinguishes absent context, evidential context, and instructional text. The last distinction matters because a grammar instruction can mention the requested concept without providing the answer.

Exact lineage checks match public benchmark or question-answer records only when normalized prompt, response, and applicable context fields agree. Reliability-weighted answer banks can resolve a row when the accepted answer relation is unambiguous. Approximate retrieval may nominate a candidate, but cannot overrule an exact source or an intrinsic context contradiction.

\subsection{Structured and Context Solvers}

Small solvers cover arithmetic, numeric ranges, Bengali compound number words, multiple-choice serialization, morphology, compounds, and prefixes. Relation guards bind dates or numbers to the clause containing the requested entity and predicate. This prevents a valid year elsewhere in a passage from answering the wrong birth, death, publication, or establishment question.

For grounded rows, response claims are checked against the supplied passage. Exact answer spans are accepted only inside a semicolon-aware clause that also contains prompt anchors. Named entities, dates, negation, and alternatives are checked before external facts may contribute. Supplied context takes precedence over outside plausibility.

\subsection{Evidence Admission and Conflict Resolution}

Table~\ref{tab:evidence} summarizes the admission policy. Idiom records distinguish figurative meaning from literal gloss, a known weakness in Bengali LLMs \citep{idiom}. Legal records require the named act and section. A free-form fact requires two independent public sources and a typed relation. These guards were added after broad retrieval repeatedly found relevant passages that answered a neighboring question.

\begin{table}[t]
\caption{Evidence admission policy.}
\label{tab:evidence}
\centering
\small
\begin{tabularx}{\columnwidth}{@{}p{0.29\columnwidth}X@{}}
\toprule
Evidence family & Requirement for emitting a label \\
\midrule
Exact lineage & Prompt, response, and applicable context agree \\

Structured rule & Parsed values, entity, and predicate align \\
Context evidence & Same clause and prompt anchors; no contradiction \\

Fuzzy retrieval & Candidate generation only \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabularx}
\end{table}

Every proposal records source family, relation, and confidence. Intrinsic context contradiction has highest priority. Exact authoritative lineage follows, then relation-aligned parsing and consensus evidence. If sources describe different relations, neither decides the row. If they claim the same relation but disagree, the system requires a stronger exact source or abstains. We accepted a larger judge queue when that was safer than allowing weak retrieval to override a trusted decision.

\subsection{Selective Open-Weight Judge}

The final Phase 1 router leaves 158 rows unresolved. They are sorted by row ID and evaluated in fixed batches. Context rows receive one entailment view. No-context rows receive up to three short views: factual verification, strict exam grading, and a zero-bias check. A faithful decision requires unanimous positive votes; evaluation stops after the first zero.

The judge is Qwen3.6-27B Q4\_K\_P, a 16.332~GiB GGUF checkpoint, served by pinned \texttt{llama.cpp} b9637 across two T4 GPUs \citep{qwen,llamacpp}. Thinking is disabled, and a grammar restricts generation to one binary token. Seeds, row order, prompt order, and batch boundaries are fixed.

\section{Experiments}

\subsection{Protocol}

We used the 299 released labels for validation and error analysis, not as proof that a large fine-tune would generalize. We tracked macro-F1, per-class F1, accuracy, deterministic coverage, and context/no-context behavior. A candidate rule entered the system only when it expressed a reusable relation and passed the regression suite. The public leaderboard represented roughly 30\% of Phase 1, so a rounded score change was treated as aggregate evidence rather than a row label.

\subsection{Iteration and Error Analysis}

Figure~\ref{fig:progress} and Table~\ref{tab:versions} summarize the experiment path. Character and embedding models established a 0.657 baseline. Gemma 12B QAT and the Qwen judge improved factual reasoning but remained prompt-sensitive. v6 introduced source and structured routing and jumped to 0.903. Later gains came from lineage audits, stricter provenance, legal evidence, and relation guards rather than a larger model.

Several plausible approaches failed. A prompt-vote ensemble reached 0.8512 locally but only 0.783 publicly, exposing prompt overfitting. A TF-IDF verifier improved from 0.7879 to 0.8261 out of fold, yet remained weak on no-context cultural facts. Raw external-QA retrieval often found adjacent passages with the wrong relation or authority. A global substring rule confused instructional grammar context with evidence. Finally, v87 made two externally plausible fact edits and regressed from 0.966 to 0.965. We reverted it and tightened evidence admission.

This sequence changed how experiments were selected. Retrieval became proposal-only, context rules became relation-aware, and researched facts required both provenance and a typed predicate. Candidate batches without a defensible expected direction were quarantined rather than submitted.

\subsection{Results}

Table~\ref{tab:results} separates released-label validation from leaderboard results. The full cascade makes two errors on 299 released labels; both concern internally inconsistent controls, which we did not patch with row-specific exceptions. The deterministic subset covers 225 rows with no error. On Phase 1, v91 finished public rank 5 at 0.967 and private rank 1 at 0.982. The private improvement suggests that the conservative policy transferred beyond the public slice, but it does not prove that each of the five v91 changes generalized individually.

\section{Reproducibility and Phase 2}

\subsection{Frozen Inference and Determinism}

The notebook reads the organizer's literal path:

\begin{quote}
\small\ttfamily
/kaggle/input/competitions/\\
bengali-hallucination/test set.csv
\end{quote}

It does not search for alternate filenames, branch on row count, call an external API, or load a saved Phase 1 prediction vector. The organizer can replace the file mounted at that path without editing a variable.

Reproduction requires more than a seed. The notebook sorts unresolved IDs, fixes batch boundaries, pins both \texttt{llama.cpp} server commands, waits for health checks, and assigns each view to the same server. The CSV writer preserves incoming IDs and a fixed \code{id,label} column order. Three full runs produced byte-identical files matching scored submission 54836339.

\subsection{External Resources}

The evidence inventory combines public Bengali resources rather than treating any one corpus as universal truth. QA lineage came from TyDi QA, Suman Bengali QA, BUFFET, SQuAD-BN, BanglaRQA, IndicQA, Aya Indic, and public Bengali QA collections \citep{tydi,sumanqa,buffet,squadbn,banglarqa,indicqa,ayaindic,qa70k}. Domain evidence included IndicXNLI, Bengali Wikipedia, textbooks, newspapers, Bangladesh legal corpora, history and literature QA, and public exam banks \citep{indicxnli,bnwiki,banglatextbook,banglanews,legalacts,lawqna,preindependence,bonolota,literatureqa,banglammlu,bcsqs,bcs1040}. Bengali idiom and lexical records came from Bangla Bagdhara, BLUCK, BengaliDictionary, and a word-analogy collection \citep{bagdhara,bluck,bengalidict,wordanalogy}. Wikidata supplied only corroborating structured facts \citep{wikidata}. Sources with unclear redistribution terms were used for audit or retrieval, not copied into the public package.

\subsection{Held-Out Domain Shift}

The 5,000-row run duplicates the current source mixture and therefore measures throughput, not domain accuracy. The organizer preview instead names Common Crawl, Bengali Wikipedia, Banglapedia, government service pages, recent newspapers, legislation, NCTB textbooks, and Bengali literature \citep{phase2}. Exact Phase 1 lineage should cover less of this distribution. Table~\ref{tab:shift} states the fallback policy.

Unfamiliar sources bypass lineage and reach context reasoning or the judge; approximate matches are never forced. Runtime rises as deterministic coverage falls, but the all-uncertain estimate remains below nine hours.

No fine-tuned checkpoint is required. The package pins the public Qwen quantization, CUDA runtime, evidence dataset, and inference notebook. The router contains short, citation-backed facts from public sources, but no hidden labels or manually assigned row-label vector. Every output is recomputed from the input row and frozen assets.

\section{Conclusion}

The largest gain came from changing the unit of reasoning, not enlarging the model. Exact provenance and typed solvers handled cases with clear relations; the fixed judge handled the uncertain tail. This yielded a first-place private Phase 1 score, reproducible output, and substantial headroom under the Phase 2 runtime limit. The system also exposes why each deterministic decision was made and when it chose to abstain.

\section*{Limitations}

The private score does not guarantee equal accuracy on the broader Phase 2 source mix. Exact lineage cannot replace general reasoning on unfamiliar civic, legal, textbook, and news content, and the 299 released labels are too small for reliable per-domain estimates. The all-uncertain timing is a projection, not an adversarial run. Evidence can age, conflict, or carry licensing constraints; high-stakes civic use still requires human escalation.
